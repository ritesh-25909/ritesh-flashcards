Flashcards by Ritesh Das

1. Which of the following is a type of asexual reproduction in Spirogyra?

a) Budding
b) Fragmentation
c) Binary fission
d) Spore formation

Answer: b) Fragmentation

2. Which plant reproduces through leaf in vegetative propagation?

a) Rose
b) Dahlia
c) Bryophyllum
d) Potato

Answer: c) Bryophyllum

3. Grafting is commonly used in the propagation of which plant?

a) Rice
b) Mango
c) Apple
d) Cactus

Answer: c) Apple

4. Which is the male reproductive part of a flower?

a) Ovary
b) Gynoecium
c) Corolla
d) Androecium

Answer: d) Androecium

5. Which layer of the anther wall nourishes the developing pollen grains?

a) Epidermis
b) Middle layer
c) Tapetum
d) Endothecium

Answer: c) Tapetum

6. The outer layer of the pollen grain is made of:

a) Cellulose
b) Sporopollenin
c) Pectin
d) Lignin

Answer: b) Sporopollenin

7. Which part of the ovule acts as the opening for entry of pollen tube?

a) Chalaza
b) Hilum
c) Micropyle
d) Funiculus

Answer: c) Micropyle

8. The development of embryo from a single megaspore is called:

a) Apospory
b) Apogamy
c) Monosporic
d) Polyembryony

Answer: c) Monosporic

9. How many cells and nuclei are present in a mature embryo sac?

a) 6 cells, 8 nuclei
b) 7 cells, 8 nuclei
c) 8 cells, 8 nuclei
d) 7 cells, 7 nuclei

Answer: b) 7 cells, 8 nuclei

10. Pollination by water is known as:

a) Entomophily
b) Anemophily
c) Hydrophily
d) Ornithophily

Answer: c) Hydrophily

11. Autogamy refers to:

a) Cross-pollination
b) Self-pollination within same flower
c) Pollination between different species
d) Asexual reproduction

Answer: b) Self-pollination within same flower

12. Geitonogamy is functionally cross-pollination but genetically:

a) Like apomixis
b) Like autogamy
c) Like syngamy
d) Like xenogamy

Answer: b) Like autogamy

13. Pollination in Vallisneria is an example of:

a) Hypohydrophily
b) Epihydrophily
c) Entomophily
d) Anemophily

Answer: b) Epihydrophily

14. Which of these prevents self-pollination?

a) Homogamy
b) Cleistogamy
c) Herkogamy
d) Chasmogamy

Answer: c) Herkogamy

15. The fusion of male gamete and secondary nucleus is called:

a) Syngamy
b) Triple fusion
c) Fertilization
d) Gametogenesis

Answer: b) Triple fusion

16. Double fertilization is a characteristic feature of:

a) Gymnosperms
b) Bryophytes
c) Angiosperms
d) Algae

Answer: c) Angiosperms

17. Endosperm formed without cell walls initially is of which type?

a) Helobial
b) Cellular
c) Nuclear
d) Mosaic

Answer: c) Nuclear

18. What is the ploidy of endosperm in angiosperms?

a) Haploid
b) Diploid
c) Triploid
d) Tetraploid

Answer: c) Triploid

19. In monocot seeds, the single cotyledon is called:

a) Plumule
b) Coleoptile
c) Scutellum
d) Coleorhiza

Answer: c) Scutellum

20. Which fruit is naturally parthenocarpic?

a) Apple
b) Banana
c) Mango
d) Grape

Answer: b) Banana

21. Which hormone is involved in inducing parthenocarpy?

a) Cytokinin
b) ABA
c) IAA (Auxin)
d) Ethylene

Answer: c) IAA (Auxin)

22. Embryo development without fertilization is known as:

a) Apomixis
b) Syngamy
c) Triple fusion
d) Fertilization

Answer: a) Apomixis

23. Polyembryony was first discovered in which plant genus?

a) Mangifera
b) Citrus
c) Nicotiana
d) Capsella

Answer: b) Citrus

24. Seed in coconut is:

a) Non-albuminous
b) Albuminous
c) Viviparous
d) Polyembryonic

Answer: b) Albuminous

25. Micropyle helps in:

a) Formation of embryo
b) Entry of pollen tube
c) Growth of cotyledons
d) Absorption of water only

Answer: b) Entry of pollen tube

26. Chalazogamy refers to entry of pollen tube through:

a) Micropyle
b) Chalaza
c) Hilum
d) Funicle

Answer: b) Chalaza

27. The fusion of two polar nuclei forms:

a) Syngamy
b) Zygote
c) Secondary nucleus
d) Triploid endosperm

Answer: c) Secondary nucleus

28. The product of fertilization between male gamete and egg is:

a) Endosperm
b) Embryo
c) Ovule
d) Fruit

Answer: b) Embryo

29. The function of filiform apparatus is to:

a) Prevent pollen entry
b) Nourish the zygote
c) Guide pollen tube
d) Protect ovule

Answer: c) Guide pollen tube

30. Which of the following is not involved in artificial hybridization?

a) Emasculation
b) Bagging
c) Scion grafting
d) Hand pollination

Answer: c) Scion grafting
